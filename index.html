Water Footprint of Food Production

How can we use less water and still eat healthy?

It takes a large amount of water and energy to produce the foods we eat every day.
According to the UN, agriculture accounts for around 70% of global freshwater withdrawals, and by 2030 the demand for water will exceed supply by 40%.

The water footprint of any food product is how much freshwater is needed to produce some amount of that food.
Knowing the water footprint of the foods you choose to eat can help you better understand how some of your daily choices impact water resources and the environment.

Some foods take much more water to produce than others.
